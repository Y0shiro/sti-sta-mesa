Overview of the Programming Strand

Information Communication and Technology or ICT Strand is one of the strands offered under Technical-Vocational Livelihood (TVL) Track of
K-12 curriculum. ICT strand subjects seek to teach students concepts and skills in information technology.


ICT in Senior High School equips students with the skills and knowledge they need to qualify for TESDA-backed certifications such as the
Certificate of Competency (COC) and National Certifications (NC). These ICT strand courses ensure that TVL track graduates of the ICT a
strand in SHS can apply for IT jobs straight out of high school.







The Objectives of the Programming Strand


The ICT strand in SHS is designed to equip senior high school students with the necessary technical skills to tackle the IT industry. Graduates are expected to be able to do the following after receiving their education:
  • Utilize industry standard equipment and to think critically and solve problems involving software, hardware, and other digital domains
  • Be confident and familiar with standard tech and software necessary to carry out tasks expected of them in order to contribute meaningfully to their respective teams
  • To apply their knowledge with decisiveness and experience when pursuing further education or applying oneself in a profession fresh out of senior high school





Advantages of the Programming
Strand


Enrolling in the ICT strand for SHS gives senior high school graduates
valuable knowledge that enables them to either dive immediately into
technical professions befitting their interests and specializations,
or to pursue further studies aligned with their track. Here are some
valuable advantages of pursuing the ICT strand in SHS:
  • Graduates can confidently jump into the path of their choosing knowing
    that their track has given them ample preparation — whether they
    want to build specializations by taking up further studies or they wish
    to begin working as soon as possible
  • Senior high school students in this track receive valuable experience
    in practical applications of their knowledge which they can maximize
    in both solo and team projects, shaping them valuable members of any
    workplace
  • Students can build a better appreciation of the advancing technology of
    today, while being exposed to opportunities that encourage them to think
    about how they can safeguard and contribute to one of the world’s most
    dynamic industries.





Programming Strand Courses
Senior high school graduates of the Programming strand can enroll in the following undergraduate programs:
  • Computer Engineering
  • Information Technology
  • Software and Network Engineering
  • Multimedia Arts
  • Digital Illustrations and Animations
  • Graphic Arts
